Now I programme computers as a hobby. I have used a programming language called Scratch, which uses blocks that you join together. You can see a Scratch program on the right. The program makes the clown blink, makes his nose light up and spins his bowtie when you press buttons and turn knobs.

The smallest computer that I have is a Raspberry Pi. I expect that Rowan's Daddy and Austin's Daddy have a Raspberry Pi. You can see my Pi on the desk in a clear plastic box just behind the keyboard.

I don't have a screen and keyboard to plug into the Pi, so I have connected it with a cable to my Mac computer. I can then use the Mac's keyboard and mouse and the Pi's desktop is shown in the window on the screen. Within the Pi desktop you can see a browser window displaying my simple web page that I talked about on an earlier page.

Later I plugged the Pi into our wi-fi router (via our electricity cables and two little boxes that Austin's Daddy gave me) so that it could use the Internet.
